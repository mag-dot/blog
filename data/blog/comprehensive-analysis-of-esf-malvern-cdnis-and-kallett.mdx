### Additional Comparison Notes- **ESF** remains one of the more affordable and consistently high-performing international school networks in Hong Kong, with stable, strong IB results and a very high pass rate.​

- **Malvern College Hong Kong** is newer but rapidly competitive, with a rising IB average (39) and strong university placements. However, capital levies and higher fees apply from preschool onward.​

- **CDNIS** is a top choice for IB, with consistent perfect scores and a high percentage achieving 40+, also offering Canadian curriculum integration.​

- **Kellett** is a British international school, offering IGCSEs and A-levels (not IB) with very high rates of A*-A results (85% at GCSE in 2025) and a strong reputation for UK university placements.​

- **Victoria Shanghai Academy (VSA)** is another strong IB school, included for context. It is locally popular, with a high average, and a large proportion of its cohort scoring 40+.​

### Practical Insights- For parents seeking the highest IB results, Malvern, CDNIS, and VSA are close competitors, with ESF slightly lower but still excellent and generally more cost-effective.[esf+3](https://www.esf.edu.hk/public-exam-results/)​

- Kellett is best for those focused on a UK academic pathway, with exceptional IGCSE/A-level results.[alumni.kellettschool+1](https://alumni.kellettschool.com/news/school-news/513/513-GCSE-and-A-level-Results-)​

- Upfront fees, application charges, capital levies, and debentures vary by school and can add substantial costs. Always check the latest fee schedule and additional mandatory charges before applying.[cdnis+3](https://www.cdnis.edu.hk/admissions/tuition-fees)​
